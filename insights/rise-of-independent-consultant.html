The Rise of the Independent Consultant: Why Businesses Are Rethinking Big Consulting Firms

By Artyom Pugachev
8 min read
Related Services
  • Market Sizing
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Demand Validation

For decades, top-tier consulting firms—McKinsey, Bain, and BCG (MBB)—were the default choice for companies tackling complex challenges. Their credibility, structured methodologies, and access to elite talent made them indispensable. Yet something is shifting.

Businesses are increasingly bypassing them in favor of independent consultants—often former MBB employees—who offer the same strategic acumen with greater agility and deeper collaboration.

This shift is not just about cost. Companies today want impact, not reports. They value fast execution, hands-on involvement, and sustainable change—areas where traditional firms, with their rigid structures and advisory-heavy approach, often fall short.

Companies want impact, not reports.

MBB still dominates high-stakes, large-scale transformations, but for many businesses, the reflexive reliance on big consulting firms is fading.

The Freelancer's Edge: Speed, Agility, and Lasting Impact

Hiring a big consulting firm takes time—extensive scoping, contracts, and a structured approach that often slows down execution. In contrast, independent consultants can be engaged in days, adapt quickly, and integrate seamlessly into a company's workflow.

But speed is just the beginning. What's also tipping the scales further in their favor is access to information and technology. The traditional advantage of large firms—exclusive research, proprietary industry benchmarks, and deep expert networks—is eroding.

Today, freelancers leverage the same expert networks, have access to AI tools, and market intelligence platforms as their former employers.

Freelancers don't just advise—they execute. Large consultancies often produce high-level recommendations that require internal teams to implement. Freelancers, on the other hand, embed themselves within organizations, working alongside teams to ensure solutions are not just designed but operationalized.

What This Means for Businesses

The rise of independent consulting doesn't mean big firms are obsolete. For massive transformations requiring dozens of consultants and global coordination, MBB still makes sense. But for many strategic challenges, businesses now have a choice.

Consider what you actually need:

  • Speed to insight: If you need answers in weeks rather than months, an independent consultant can mobilize faster.
  • Execution support: If the bottleneck is implementation rather than strategy, someone who works alongside your team may deliver more value than a deck of recommendations.
  • Specialized expertise: For niche markets or technical domains, a focused independent often brings deeper knowledge than a generalist team.
  • Senior attention: With an independent, you get the senior person—not a rotating cast of analysts supervised by a partner who appears monthly.

The Accountability Difference

There's another factor that rarely gets discussed: accountability structures are different. Large consulting firms optimize for many things—utilization rates, partner economics, brand protection—and client outcomes are only one input in that equation.

An independent consultant's reputation is their business. Every engagement is a direct reflection of their capabilities. There's nowhere to hide behind a brand name or a team. This creates a different kind of accountability—one that often translates to deeper commitment to results.

The Bottom Line

The consulting industry is evolving. The question isn't whether big firms or independents are "better"—it's which model fits your specific situation. For many companies, the answer is increasingly: we want senior expertise, fast execution, and real accountability. That's exactly what the best independent consultants deliver.

The firms that built the consulting industry will continue to thrive. But they're no longer the only option—and for a growing number of businesses, they're not the best one.

Key Takeaway

Independent consultants offer what many large firms struggle to deliver: senior expertise from day one, fast execution, and direct accountability for results.

For companies seeking strategic insight without the overhead of big consulting, the independent model is no longer an alternative—it's often the better choice.
